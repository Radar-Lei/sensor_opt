SUMMARY OF CONTRIBUTIONS
------------------------

This paper addresses the fundamental problem of optimally placing sparse traffic
sensors to enable accurate, transparent, and certifiable reconstruction of
full-network traffic states. Our contributions are as follows:

1. We formulate sensor placement as a bilevel network design problem, where the
   upper level selects sensor locations to minimize reconstruction uncertainty
   and the lower level performs transparent, closed-form traffic state estimation
   via generalized least squares (GLS) and graph signal processing (GSP) methods.

2. We develop a reconstruction-aware candidate selection (RCSS) framework that
   integrates posterior-trace certificates, scenario-based robustness scores, and
   validation-aware swap refinement, enabling data-driven selection among diverse
   candidate layouts without requiring black-box surrogate models.

3. We provide theoretical analysis of the posterior error certificate, establishing
   conditions under which the certificate serves as a reliable predictor of
   out-of-sample reconstruction accuracy.

4. We conduct extensive computational experiments on three benchmark networks
   (PeMS7 with 228 nodes, PeMS7 with 1,026 nodes, and the Seattle heterogeneous
   highway network), demonstrating that our approach consistently outperforms
   established baselines including random, degree-based, variance-based, and
   coverage-based placement strategies, with statistically significant improvements
   confirmed through paired tests.

SUITABILITY FOR TRANSPORTATION RESEARCH PART B
-----------------------------------------------

We believe this manuscript is well suited for Transportation Research Part B:
Methodological for the following reasons:

- The paper contributes a principled optimization methodology for a core
  transportation system design problem (sensor network design), which aligns
  with TR-B's focus on methodological advances in transportation.

- The bilevel optimization formulation, posterior certificate theory, and robust
  candidate selection framework constitute significant methodological innovations
  that extend beyond incremental empirical improvements.

- The rigorous computational experimentation across multiple real-world networks,
  combined with statistical validation and ablation studies, meets the high
  evidence standards expected by the journal.

- The transparent, closed-form nature of the reconstruction framework offers
  practical advantages for transportation agencies that require interpretable
  and auditable sensor placement decisions.
