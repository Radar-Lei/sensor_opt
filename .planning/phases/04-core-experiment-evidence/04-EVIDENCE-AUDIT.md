## Locked Claim Implications

- Strong performance wording can be preserved for PeMS7_228 where it cites held-out GLS/MAP Stage 12 evidence and names the supported baselines directly.
- Validation MAE is not final performance evidence. It may explain `best_random_by_validation`, RCSS weight tuning, or validation-swap selection, but manuscript comparisons must use held-out GLS/MAP rows from curated `metrics.csv` / `combined_metrics.csv` artifacts.
- The PeMS7_228 10% multistart result remains a visible caveat and predeclared comparator issue. It should be reported directly rather than hidden by selecting whichever method wins by budget after observing test outcomes.
- PeMS7_1026 is lower-power external evidence, and Seattle is supporting/conditional evidence. These are the only WARN statuses allowed by the final validator.
- Certificate correlations support certificate-guided selection empirically; formal “certified” language remains out of scope unless theory is added elsewhere.
- EXP-06 Stage 13 supports practical runtime/candidate-count tractability language only; broader robustness and scalability claims belong to later phases.
